How to Use the Dashboard

"Hello Everyone! Welcome to F1 Dashboard. If you are stuck anywhere, please read that section's tips and guide. I highly recommend reading through it at least once—you might discover a helpful feature you would have otherwise overlooked." - Cheers, Parth

🏎️ Live Telemetry & Replays

  • Viewing a Session: Navigate to the Live Telemetry page. If a race is currently live, the system will detect it automatically and display real-time data.
  • Replaying Past Races: If no race is live, use the "Select Session" dropdown in the Telemetry Replay Control panel to choose a completed Grand Prix.
  • Playback Controls: Select your preferred playback speed (1x, 5x, or 10x) and click Start Replay. You can pause and resume the action at any time using the corresponding buttons.
  • Interacting with Data: Hover your mouse over any driver in the leaderboard to highlight their position on the track map. The table updates dynamically with their speed, current tires, gap to the leader, and pit stops.

🏆 Standings

  • Switching Views: On the Standings page, use the toggle switch at the top to flip seamlessly between Drivers and Teams (Constructors).
  • Team Colors: Each driver and constructor is color-coded with their official team colors for quick, at-a-glance identification.

📅 Race Schedule

  • Track Details: The Schedule page displays the full race calendar for the current season.
  • Interactive Cards: Click anywhere on a race card (on the track name or flag) to visit the official circuit information page.
  • Maps Integration: Click the small map pin icon at the bottom of any race card to instantly open the exact circuit location in Google Maps.

👤 Fan Profile

  • Personalization: Enter your name and bio to customize your experience.
  • Selecting Drivers: In the "Favourite Drivers" list, hold down CTRL (on Windows) or CMD (on Mac) while clicking to select multiple drivers at once.
  • Alerts: Check the boxes to opt-in to simulated Red Flag and Safety Car alerts to customize your dashboard notifications.
